PART-TIME JOB

Part-Time Jobs are employment positions in which an individual works for fewer hours than a full-time employee. These jobs usually involve flexible working hours and may not require a long-term commitment.

Part-time jobs are commonly suitable for students, freelancers, homemakers, or individuals seeking extra income. Employees are generally paid on an hourly, daily, or monthly basis, and benefits are often limited compared to full-time jobs. In a job board application, part-time jobs help users find opportunities that match their availability and work–life balance.

FULL-TIME JOB

Full-Time Jobs are employment positions in which an individual works a fixed number of hours per week, typically 35–40 hours, for a single organization. These jobs usually involve a long-term or permanent work arrangement.

Employees in full-time jobs receive a regular salary and often enjoy additional benefits such as paid leave, health insurance, provident fund, and job security. In a job board application, full-time jobs are listed for candidates seeking stable careers and long-term professional growth.



INTERNSHIP


An Internship is a short-term, training-oriented job designed mainly for students or fresh graduates (freshers) to help them gain practical, real-world experience in a particular field.

During an internship, candidates work under the guidance of experienced professionals and apply the theoretical knowledge they have learned in college to real tasks and projects. Internships may be paid or unpaid and usually last for a few weeks to several months. They help students develop technical skills, improve communication, understand workplace culture, and enhance their resumes. In a job board application, internship listings are especially useful for candidates who are starting their careers and looking to build industry experience.



FREELANCE JOB


Freelance Jobs are a type of work where individuals are self-employed professionals who offer their skills and services to clients on a task-based or project-based basis instead of working as permanent employees.

Freelancers are hired for specific projects such as website development, graphic design, content writing, digital marketing, or data analysis. They usually work for multiple clients at the same time and have the freedom to choose their working hours and location. Payment is typically made per project, per task, or per hour. In a job board application, freelance jobs help connect companies with skilled professionals for short-term or specialized work without long-term employment commitments.
